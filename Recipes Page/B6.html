Stuffed Paratha

Stuffed Paratha is a delicious and filling Indian flatbread made with a spiced stuffing of vegetables or paneer. It is best served with yogurt, pickle, or butter!

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 tsp oil or ghee

For the Stuffing:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup grated paneer (optional)
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp dry mango powder (amchur)
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

For Cooking:

  • Ghee or oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough. Cover and set aside for 20 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, mix mashed potatoes, paneer (if using), green chilies, cumin seeds, red chili powder, garam masala, dry mango powder, salt, and coriander leaves to make the stuffing.
  3. Divide the dough into equal portions. Roll out one portion into a small circle.
  4. Place a spoonful of stuffing in the center, gather the edges, and seal it.
  5. Gently roll it out again into a thick paratha.
  6. Heat a tawa or griddle and place the rolled-out paratha on it.
  7. Cook for 1-2 minutes, flip, and apply ghee/oil on both sides.
  8. Cook until golden brown and crisp on both sides.
  9. Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or butter.
