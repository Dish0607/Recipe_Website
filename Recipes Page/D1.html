Gujhia

Gujhia is a traditional Indian sweet pastry filled with a rich mixture of khoya, nuts, and sugar. It is deep-fried to a golden crisp and is especially popular during Holi and Diwali!

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa), crumbled
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (optional)
  • 2 tbsp chopped almonds
  • 2 tbsp chopped cashews
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp raisins

For Frying:

  • Ghee or oil, for deep frying

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour and ghee. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover and set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pan, dry roast the crumbled khoya until it turns light golden. Let it cool.
  3. Mix khoya with powdered sugar, grated coconut (if using), chopped almonds, cashews, raisins, and cardamom powder.
  4. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a small circle.
  5. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, fold the dough over, and seal the edges using water or by twisting the edges.
  6. Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan and fry the gujias on medium heat until golden brown and crisp.
  7. Drain on paper towels and let them cool before serving.
  8. Enjoy Gujia with tea or as a festive treat!
