Payasam

Payasam, also known as Kheer, is a traditional South Indian dessert made with milk, rice, jaggery or sugar, and flavored with cardamom and nuts. It is often prepared during festivals and special occasions.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup rice (or vermicelli)
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 10-12 cashews
  • 10-12 almonds
  • 2 tbsp raisins

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and fry cashews, almonds, and raisins until golden brown. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add rice (or vermicelli) and sauté for 2-3 minutes until aromatic.
  3. Pour in the milk and let it simmer on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is fully cooked.
  4. Once the rice is soft, add jaggery or sugar and mix well. Stir continuously until it dissolves.
  5. Add cardamom powder and stir to enhance the flavor.
  6. Simmer for a few more minutes until the payasam thickens to your desired consistency.
  7. Finally, mix in the fried nuts and raisins.
  8. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with extra nuts. Enjoy!
