Puttu

Puttu is a traditional South Indian and Sri Lankan steamed rice cake, usually served with coconut, banana, or curry. Follow this recipe to make soft and fluffy puttu at home!

Ingredients

For the Puttu:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup warm water (adjust as needed)

For Serving (Optional):

  • Banana Leaf (Optional)
  • Jaggery
  • Kadala curry (black chickpea curry)
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the rice flour and salt. Gradually sprinkle warm water while mixing until the mixture becomes moist and crumbly.
  2. Rub the mixture between your fingers to break any lumps. The texture should resemble wet sand.
  3. Let it rest for 10 minutes to absorb moisture.
  4. In a puttu maker, layer grated coconut first, followed by the rice flour mixture. Repeat until the mold is filled, ending with a coconut layer.
  5. Steam the puttu for about 5-7 minutes until fully cooked and fluffy.
  6. Gently push the puttu out of the mold and serve warm.
  7. Enjoy with Banana Leaf (Optional), jaggery, sugar, or kadala curry!
