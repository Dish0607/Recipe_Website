Mushroom Biryani

Mushroom Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish made with mushrooms, basmati rice, and a blend of spices.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 1.5 cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Masala:

  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tbsp chopped mint leaves

Instructions

  1. Cook the Rice: Wash basmati rice and soak it for 20 minutes. Boil 3 cups water in a pot. Add bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, salt, and the soaked rice. Cook until 90% done, then drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Masala: Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and sliced onions. Sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add Spices & Mushrooms: Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute. Stir in tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  4. Cook Mushrooms: Add sliced mushrooms and cook for 3-4 minutes until they release moisture.
  5. Mix with Yogurt: Stir in yogurt, salt, chopped mint, and coriander leaves. Cook for another 3 minutes until well combined.
  6. Layering: In a large pan, layer the mushroom masala and cooked rice. Drizzle some ghee on top.
  7. Dum Cooking: Cover and cook on low flame for 10 minutes to allow flavors to blend.
  8. Serve Hot: Fluff the biryani gently and serve with raita or salad.
