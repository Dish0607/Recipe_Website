Unni Appam

Unni Appam is a traditional South Indian sweet snack made with rice, jaggery, banana, and coconut. It is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, perfect for festive occasions!

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 3/4 cup jaggery
  • 1 small ripe banana
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon dry ginger powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the raw rice for at least 2 hours. Drain and grind it into a smooth batter using little water.
  2. Melt the jaggery in a small pan with a little water until it dissolves completely. Strain to remove impurities and let it cool.
  3. Mash the ripe banana and add it to the rice batter.
  4. Mix in the grated coconut, jaggery syrup, cardamom powder, dry ginger powder, and baking soda. Stir well to form a thick batter.
  5. Heat ghee in a pan and fry the grated coconut until golden. Add this to the batter and mix well.
  6. Let the batter rest for 30 minutes to enhance the flavors.
  7. Heat oil in an Unni Appam pan (appe pan) on medium flame.
  8. Pour small amounts of batter into each cavity of the pan.
  9. Cook on low-medium heat, flipping occasionally, until golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove from the pan and drain on a paper towel.
  11. Serve warm and enjoy your homemade Unni Appam!
