Rajma Chawal

Rajma Chawal is a classic North Indian dish made with kidney beans cooked in a rich tomato-based gravy, served with steamed rice.

Ingredients

For the Rajma:

  • 1 cup rajma (kidney beans), soaked overnight
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For the Rice:

  • 1.5 cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Cook the Rajma: Drain the soaked rajma and pressure cook with 3 cups of water for 4-5 whistles until soft.
  2. Prepare the Masala: Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add Aromatics: Sauté onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add Spices: Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  6. Simmer with Rajma: Add cooked rajma along with its water. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, mashing a few beans for thickness.
  7. Final Touch: Add garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Cook the Rice: Wash and soak basmati rice for 20 minutes. Boil 3 cups water, add rice and salt, and cook until tender.
  9. Serve Hot: Plate the rice and top with hot rajma. Serve with onion salad or yogurt.
