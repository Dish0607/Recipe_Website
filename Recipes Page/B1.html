Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian crispy crepe filled with a spiced potato filling. Follow this recipe to make delicious masala dosa from scratch!

Ingredients

For the batter:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (optional, for crispiness)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, to taste

For cooking:

  • Oil or ghee, for greasing

For the potato filling:

  • 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Few curry leaves
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

Prepare the batter:

  1. Rinse the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds thoroughly. Soak them in water for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them in a blender or wet grinder, adding water gradually to form a smooth, thick batter.
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover it, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it doubles in volume and turns slightly airy.
  4. Once fermented, add salt and mix the batter gently without deflating the air bubbles.

Prepare the potato filling:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add cumin seeds, curry leaves, chopped onions, and green chili. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  3. Add turmeric powder and mix well.
  4. Add the mashed potatoes and salt. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander and set aside.

Make the masala dosa:

  1. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
  2. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the pan and quickly spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  3. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  4. Place a spoonful of the prepared potato filling in the center of the dosa.
  5. Fold the dosa over the filling and press gently.
  6. Remove the dosa and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
